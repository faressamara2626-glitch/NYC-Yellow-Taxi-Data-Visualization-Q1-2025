# NYC Yellow Taxi Data Visualization – Q1 2025

## Overview
This project is an executive data visualization report analyzing NYC Yellow Taxi operations from January to March 2025.

The report is based on 11.2 million yellow taxi trips and focuses on geographic demand, peak demand times, revenue patterns, payment behavior, tipping behavior, and the impact of the CBD congestion fee.

## Project Goal
The goal of this project is to turn large-scale taxi trip data into clear business insights and practical recommendations for fleet positioning, staffing, revenue optimization, and operational planning.

## Data Source
The analysis uses NYC Taxi and Limousine Commission (TLC) yellow taxi trip data for Q1 2025.

## Main Analysis Areas
- Pickup and dropoff demand by taxi zone
- Top pickup and dropoff locations
- Hourly and weekday demand patterns
- Revenue composition by month
- Relationship between trip distance and total fare
- Airport pickups versus city trips
- Vendor comparison
- Payment type distribution
- Passenger count distribution
- Credit card tipping behavior
- CBD congestion fee impact

## Key Insights
- Midtown Manhattan is the strongest pickup and dropoff demand area.
- LaGuardia Airport is a major high-value zone and should be prioritized for airport allocation.
- Demand peaks during morning hours and is strongest during evening hours.
- Thursday and Friday evenings are among the most valuable demand windows.
- March demand and revenue were higher than January.
- Airport trips generate much higher revenue and tips than regular city trips.
- Credit card payments dominate the payment mix.
- Cash tips are not fully visible in the data.
- The CBD congestion fee did not create a clear demand shock after launch.

## Business Recommendations
- Concentrate vehicles in Midtown Center and Midtown East during peak periods.
- Prioritize LaGuardia Airport for dedicated airport allocation.
- Use separate shift patterns for weekday commuter demand and weekend late-night demand.
- Increase staffing from January to March instead of treating all months equally.
- Promote card payments to improve tip visibility.
- Investigate disputed trips as a service-quality signal.
- Treat CBD fees as pass-through charges, not operator income.
